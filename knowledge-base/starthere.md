# Start Here

The go-to-market motion from zero, in order. Each step has what it is, why it matters, and your first
move. Work top to bottom; do not skip ahead. The deeper how-to for each step lives in Plays.

Format (parsed by `app/scripts/build-data.mjs`): `## N. Title`, then `- what/why/action/see:` lines.

## 1. Pick your beachhead
- what: Choose the one narrow segment you can actually win in the next 3 to 18 months, not the whole market.
- why: Everything downstream (positioning, channel, message) gets sharper when it is aimed at one specific buyer. Trying to serve everyone is why most early GTM stalls.
- action: Write your target in three attributes that genuinely narrow it, for example "Series A, marketplace, US".
- see: Plays, Define your beachhead (ECP before ICP)

## 2. Choose your motion
- what: Decide how you go to market: product-led self-serve, sales-led, account-based, or a hybrid.
- why: The motion has to fit your price. A $200/mo product cannot fund a high-touch sales team; a $50k product will not sell itself.
- action: Use the Advisor: enter your ACV and read the recommended motion and channels.
- see: Advisor; Plays, Pick your motion by ACV

## 3. Nail your positioning
- what: State why a buyer should pick you, both against the alternatives and against doing nothing.
- why: Positioning is the input to every message, page, and pitch. Get it vague and everything downstream is vague.
- action: Fill in two sentences: "We are the [X] of [Y]" and "We remove the risk of [the pain]". Lead with risk removed, not upside.
- see: Plays, Nail your positioning

## 4. Design your offer
- what: Define exactly what you sell: the outcome, who it is for, and the mechanism that makes it work.
- why: A clear, specific offer converts. A vague "we help with X" does not give a buyer a reason to act now.
- action: Write the offer in one sentence: "For [beachhead], we [deliver outcome] by [mechanism]."
- see: Plays, Design your offer

## 5. Choose your first channel
- what: Pick the single channel that best reaches your beachhead. One channel, not five.
- why: You learn nothing from running everything at once. One channel, run properly, gives you a clean signal.
- action: Pick the one highest-fit channel for your motion (for low ACV: warm or signal-based outbound, or SEO; not paid yet).
- see: Plays, Choose your channel

## 6. Build your first list
- what: Assemble the actual accounts and the right contacts at them.
- why: Outbound is only as good as the list. The best message to the wrong people does nothing.
- action: Build a 100-row list of your beachhead with verified contacts. Do it by hand the first time so you learn what good looks like.
- see: Plays, Build your first list

## 7. Write the message
- what: Craft outreach that is relevant to a specific situation, not a generic template.
- why: Relevance earns replies; generic earns deletes. The point is to say something true and useful that only applies to them.
- action: Draft one message you would actually reply to. Tie it to a real signal and offer something of value upfront.
- see: Plays, Run signal-based outbound; Write a permissionless value message

## 8. Set up sending infrastructure
- what: Stand up the domains, authentication, and warmup that let cold email reach the inbox.
- why: For outbound, deliverability often decides results more than copy. Skip this and nobody sees your message.
- action: Set up a separate sending domain with SPF, DKIM, and DMARC, and warm the mailboxes before sending.
- see: Plays, Set up cold-email deliverability; Stay compliant

## 9. Run a fixed-window test
- what: Validate one channel and message over a fixed window before you scale or automate anything.
- why: Scaling an unproven motion just multiplies a mistake. Test small, judge on real outcomes, then commit.
- action: Send your first 10 messages by hand and read every reply. Judge on meetings booked, not opens.
- see: Plays, Run a fixed-window outbound test

## 10. Measure what matters
- what: Track the few metrics that actually predict pipeline, and know what "good" looks like.
- why: You cannot improve what you do not measure, and most teams optimize vanity metrics that do not matter.
- action: Pick one leading indicator to watch (for example reply rate or meetings booked) and compare it to the benchmarks.
- see: Plays, Measure what matters; Benchmarks

## 11. Scale what works
- what: Automate the step that proved itself, then add volume, channels, and segments.
- why: Scale is for validated motions only. Automate the proven step, not the whole thing, and keep a human in the loop.
- action: Take the one step that earned replies and automate just that, then expand to the next segment.
- see: Plays, Build a GTM system with skills
